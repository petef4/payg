PAYG Tariffs

These are the rates for true pay as you go tariffs only. Some other plans are labelled PAYG but require monthly top-ups, etc. Plans which are not open to new customers are also excluded.

If you are happy to pay a certain amount each month, see Ken's Top Tips for the best deals and other phone info. That site also has a comparison tool covering most of the available plans. They also have paygvscontract.com comparing smartphone tariffs.

Details such as rates for non-geographic numbers (e.g. 0845) are not included here. Discounts are often available when committing to some usage (aka add-ons or bolt-ons). Many plans give free credits when a top-up is made but these tend to be valid for 30 days only. Extra services are available, some of which are free. Follow the links for individual plans to find out more. Some of the better hidden fine print can be found in the plan details links of the operators column.

The five main operators who operate their own networks are O2, Orange, T-Mobile, Vodafone and 3. The first four operate 2G services primarily and are moving towards 3G. 3 operates 3G and uses Orange to provide its 2G coverage. Orange and T-Mobile are merging their UK operations as "Everything Everywhere"; their 2G networks are shared via roaming. 3, Orange and T-Mobile have a joint venture MBNL to combine their 3G networks.

MVNOs are operators who do not run their own network. The ones who target UK usage are Asda, Delight Mobile, giffgaff, Family (IKEA), Talkmobile (Carphone Warehouse), Tesco, Utility Warehouse and Virgin. Those geared towards international plans include Dalya, Econet (formerly IDT), GT Mobile, iCard, Lebara, Lycamobile, Now Mobile, Vectone and Vizz. Tru is pitching itself as a roaming MVNO.

The Network column has links to coverage maps. The GSMA also has coverage maps for all the 2G and 3G networks.

The cheapest for low usage voice and text are Family Mobile, Tesco Lite and Talkmobile Essential.

Many plans give free credit on each top-up that lasts for 30 days, though often restricted to their own numbers. The footnotes show exceptions. The best are Delight and Tesco PAYG which offer triple credit. Tesco has a minimum top-up of £10 and the bonus credit expires after 1 month. Delight Mobile has a minimum top-up of £5.
