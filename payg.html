PAYG Tariffs

These are the rates for true pay as you go tariffs only. Some other plans are labelled PAYG but require monthly top-ups, etc. Plans which are not open to new customers are also excluded.

If you are happy to pay a certain amount each month, see Ken's Top Tips for the best deals and other phone info. That site also has a comparison tool covering the main plans. They also have paygvscontract.com comparing smartphone tariffs.

Another site with information similar to here is Mobile Network Comparison.

Details such as rates for non-geographic numbers (e.g. 0845, 0800) are not included here. Discounts are often available when committing to some usage (aka add-ons or bolt-ons). Many plans give free credits when a top-up is made but these tend to be valid for 30 days only. Extra services are available, some of which are free. Follow the links for individual plans to find out more. Some of the better hidden fine print can be found in the plan details links of the operators column.

The five main operators who operate their own networks are O2, Orange, T-Mobile, Vodafone and 3. The first four operate 2G and 3G services and are rolling out 4G in 2013. 3 does not operate its own 2G and uses some of EE's network to provide 2G where 3G is lacking coverage. Orange and T-Mobile have merged their UK operations as EE, formerly Everything Everywhere; their 2G and 3G networks are shared via roaming.

3 and EE have a joint venture MBNL to combine their 3G networks. O2 and Vodafone have their own joint venture called Cornerstone to share masts. This should lead to two coverage maps to consider rather than five.

MVNOs are operators who do not run their own network. The ones who target UK usage are Asda, Boy Better Know, Delight Mobile, Freedom Talk, giffgaff, Family (IKEA), Ovivo, The People's Operator, RSPCA, Soccertel, Talkmobile (Carphone Warehouse), Tesco, Victory Mobile, Virgin Mobile and WWF Wildlife Mobile. Those geared towards international plans include AfriMobile, Banana Mobile, C Mobile, DiDa Mobile, DobryTel, Econet, GT Mobile, iCard, Kite, Kontakt, Lebara, Lycamobile, Now Mobile, Simfiniti, Simple Call, SMART Pinoy, Talk Home, Vectone, Vizz and White Mobile. Maxroam, toggle and Truphone are pitching themselves as roaming MVNOs. Prepaid MVNO reports that there are 92 MVNOs as of May 2013, and Telecompaper lists 54, though not all offer PAYG.

The Network column has links to coverage maps. The GSMA and UK Mobile Coverage also have coverage maps for all the 2G and 3G networks. It is unclear whether EE's Smart Signal Sharing applies to all its MVNOs yet.

The cheapest for low usage voice and text are Family Mobile, Kontakt, Tesco Lite, Talkmobile Essential, Talk Home and White Mobile. Cheaper still is Ovivo which is effectively free if you don't mind targeted advertising. The plan which I use is Talkmobile Essential.

Many plans give free credit on each top-up that lasts for 30 days, though often restricted to their own numbers. The footnotes show exceptions. Best is Kontakt7. Next best is Tesco PAYG which offers triple credit. Tesco has a minimum top-up of £10 and the bonus credit expires after 1 month.

Minimum call charges are generally one minute. The exception is Kontakt who have no minimum charge and time by six second intervals. After the first minute some operators round up call lengths to the next full minute and others charge by the second. The former effectively have higher charges than at first glance. The operators who are known to charge by the second include giffgaff, Family and Tesco.
