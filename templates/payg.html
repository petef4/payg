PAYG Tariffs

TL;DR cheapest rates are

  • Three and Delight — 3p / min, 2p / text, 1p / MB.

Other cheap rates are

  • For text (3p) — The People’s Operator and Tello
  • For voice (3p) — Tello or toggle
  • For free voicemail — Call&Give, GT Mobile, Kingdom, Lomo, Lycamobile, Now, Pebble, Talk Home and White
  • For minimum call charge (1p) — Co-operative and Virgin
  • For data (1p / MB) — The People’s Operator and Tello

The plan which I use is Talkmobile Essentials4

These are the rates for true pay as you go tariffs only. Some other plans are labelled PAYG but require monthly top-ups, etc. Plans which are not open to new customers are also excluded. So Orange and T-Mobile are no longer listed as EE is the only brand available.

Discounts are often available when committing to some usage (aka add-ons or bolt-ons). Many plans give free credits when a top-up is made but these tend to be valid for 30 days only. Extra services are available, some of which are free. Follow the links for individual plans to find out more. Some of the better hidden fine print can be found in the plan details links of the operators column.

Non-geographic numbers are now regulated by Ofcom. Numbers starting 0800, 0808 and 116 are free from mobiles, as they always have been from landlines. For numbers starting 084, 087, 09 and 118 there is a cleaner charge structure. Calls to them have an access charge plus a service charge. The access charge is set by the mobile operator and is listed here in the 08x column. The service charge is set by the company you are calling.

For example, if you call 118 118 from EE, the access charge is 44p per minute and the service charge is £2.75 per call plus £2.99 per minute. So if your call is less than a minute it will cost £6.18.

The four main networks are EE, O2, Vodafone and 3. EE also runs the Orange and T-Mobile brands for old accounts. 3 and EE have a joint venture MBNL to combine their 3G networks. O2 and Vodafone have their own joint venture called Cornerstone to share masts. This should lead to two coverage maps to consider rather than four or five. BT are taking over EE and 3 is merging with O2.

MVNOs are operators who do not run their own network. The ones who target UK usage are allpay Mobile, Asda, Call&Give, co-operative mobile, Delight Mobile, giffgaff, Mobile by The People’s Operator, Post Office Mobile, Talkmobile, talkxtra, Tello, Tesco and Virgin Mobile. Those geared towards international plans include AfriMobile, CTExcelbiz, Econet, Globe UK, GT Mobile, Jadoo, Kingdom mobile, Kontakt, Lebara, Lomo Mobile, Lycamobile, Now Mobile, SMART UK, Talk Home, Vectone and White Mobile. Aether, Auracall, GO-SIM, Maxroam, Pebble, toggle and Truphone are pitching themselves as roaming MVNOs. MVNO Dynamics (formerly Prepaid MVNO) reports that there are 92 MVNOs as of May 2013, and Telecompaper lists 54, though not all offer PAYG.

There are 40 operators listed here offering 44 PAYG plans.

Coverage maps are linked to in the Network column. The GSMA have coverage maps for most of the 2G, 3G and 4G networks. It is unclear whether EE’s Smart Signal Sharing applies to all its MVNOs yet. Several operators charge a premium for 4G.

Free credit is given by many plans on each top-up. It generally lasts for 30 days, though often restricted to their own numbers. The footnotes show exceptions. Best is Kontakt5. Next best is Tesco which offers triple credit. Tesco has a minimum top-up of £10 and the bonus credit expires after 1 month.

Minimum call charges are generally one minute. The exceptions are Co-op and Virgin who have no minimum charge and time by one second intervals. After the first minute some operators round up call lengths to the next full minute and others charge by the second. The former effectively have higher charges than at first glance.
